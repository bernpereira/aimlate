About the project

A shared classroom for a borderless information age.

AI and Media Literacy: A Toolkit for Educators (AIMLATE) is a small-scale Erasmus+ partnership in school education that equips teachers and students with the AI literacy skills needed to analyse AI-generated misinformation and navigate digital content critically.

The project targets a 25% improvement in educators' AI literacy, with 80% of trained teachers integrating the toolkit into their curricula within six months, a 30% increase in students' critical thinking, and a 20% reduction in AI-driven cyberbullying and misinformation manipulation in pilot schools.

Outputs include structured lesson plans, interactive exercises, case studies, fact-checking simulations, ethical AI discussions and gamified learning modules — piloted across three EU countries.

The toolkit

Four modules. Endless classroom conversations.

Each module bundles teacher notes, learning outcomes, materials and step-by-step activities — designed for direct classroom use. The toolkit is currently under development and piloting; it is not yet publicly downloadable.

Module 01

Misinformation & Verification

Fact-checking habits, reverse image search, emotional-manipulation awareness, and verification checklists.

Module 02

AI-Generated Content & Detection

Deepfake awareness, synthetic voices, AI image clues, AI text analysis, and ethical use of generative tools.

Module 03

Algorithms, Bias & Ethics

Filter bubbles, data bias, inclusivity, and the role of AI in everyday decisions and classroom debates.

Module 04

Digital Safety & Responsible Use

Strong passwords, scams & phishing, digital footprints, cyberbullying, and responsible AI in schools.
